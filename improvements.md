## Counterpoint: the polyphony gap, concretely

Your texture is strictly melody-plus-accompaniment: one line, homophonic pad, functional bass. Four additions would fix that, in ascending order of effort:

**Parallel doubling in 3rds/6ths.** At high valence/energy, double the melody a diatonic third or sixth below (switching between the two to stay consonant against the chord). This is the cheapest polyphony in existence, reads as richness rather than as a second voice, and lints trivially (interval whitelist against the melody plus chord membership).

**Inner-voice animation.** Most of what registers as "polyphony" in media music is figurated homophony: one pad voice walks a passing tone between chord changes, or the voicing gets arpeggiated (Alberti-adjacent figuration) instead of sustained. Your voice-leading search already produces the target voicings; animating the connective tissue between them is a small generator sitting on data you already have. This also fights pad stasis at low energy, where the pad is often the only thing sounding.

**Imitation.** Restate the melody's opening cell in the arp or an upper pad voice a bar (or half-bar) later at a diatonic transposition. The motif machinery and `realize_faithful` already exist — imitation is mostly a placement policy plus a collision rule. Very high intent-per-line-of-code: the listener hears the voices *listening to each other*, which is exactly "perceived intent."

**A true countermelody generator.** Because you generate a bar ahead, the melody's IR exists when a counter-voice runs, so this can be constraint-first like everything else: consonance with the melody on strong beats (not just with the chord), preference for contrary/oblique motion, no parallel 5ths/8ves against melody or bass, and — the part that makes it sound like counterpoint rather than two melodies fighting — **rhythmic complementarity**: move where the melody holds, hold where it moves. Every one of those is a species-counterpoint rule and slots straight into `verify.py`. Gate it as a texture state the dramaturg can withhold and spend (see below).

Related and currently unguarded: **outer-voice counterpoint**. Your voicing checks are pad-internal, so nothing prevents parallel fifths/octaves between melody and bass, or both outer voices leaping the same direction into a downbeat. Theorists are right that the soprano–bass frame carries tonal music; a cheap outer-voice scan (parallels forbidden, contrary motion preferred into cadences) would raise perceived craft even before any new voice is added.

I'd also make **texture itself a Tier-2 parameter** — monophonic / homophonic / melody+doubling / melody+counter / imitative — rotated at phrase boundaries with memory (don't repeat the same texture twice, occasionally return to one from two phrases ago). Texture change is one of the strongest variety levers human arrangers use, and it gives the dramaturg a new debt currency: withholding the countermelody, then releasing it at the spend.

## Question and answer: periods

§5.5 gives you sentence form *within* a phrase, but nothing pairs phrases. The antecedent–consequent period — two phrases opening identically, the first ending on a half cadence, the second answering with an authentic one — is arguably the single strongest "a mind composed this" signal in tonal music, and it's cheap for you: phrase-pair memory plus cadence coordination, both of which the dramaturg's cadence rationing already half-implements. It also compounds with M15/M17: the signature's introduced/developed statements become the parallel openings.

## The barline problem: anacrusis and ties

M8's invariant that "raw events never cross barlines" quietly forbids two ubiquitous human gestures: the **pickup** (melodies launching from the "and of 4" into the downbeat — right now everything phrase-starts bar-aligned, which is a subtle procedural tell) and the **tied suspension across the barline** (M14's suspensions resolve at the mid-bar pulse partly because the preparation can't be held over). I'd add a `tie` flag to `NoteEvent` so a musical note can be represented as two grid-legal events; that one IR change unlocks anacrusis, cross-bar suspensions, and syncopation across the barline, and the linter can treat tied chains as single notes.

## Hypermeter and phrase-length elasticity

You weight slots within the bar but not bars within the group: bar 1 of a four-bar group is strong, bar 3 secondary. Hypermetric weight should influence where harmony changes, where crashes land, where syncopation is licensed, and where the signature slot sits. On top of it, phrase-length variety: everything is 4 or 8 bars. Humans elide (the cadence bar *is* the next phrase's bar 1 — a great high-energy device), extend (the dramaturg stretching the pre-dominant by two bars under high tension), and append **codettas** — after a big PAC, a bar of tonic prolongation echoing the last gesture. A codetta after a large dramaturg spend would make payoffs breathe instead of immediately re-entering the loop.

## Performance shaping: the "sequenced vs played" delta

Humanize is Gaussian jitter — noise, not expression. What human performance adds is *systematic* deviation tied to structure: a velocity hairpin rising to the phrase's contour peak and relaxing into the cadence; agogic lengthening of arrival notes; a micro-ritardando (1–3%) over the cadence bar with recovery at the next phrase; a *luftpause* — a few dozen milliseconds of stolen silence — before phrase downbeats; melody riding slightly behind the beat at low energy, on top at high. This is one phrase-position-aware "phrasing" modifier, fully deterministic, and I'd bet it's the highest perceived-quality-per-effort item on this whole list. Similarly, per-note velocity should partially track pitch contour (higher ≈ slightly louder), not just metric weight.

## Smaller but real gaps

Cadence-approach **harmonic acceleration**: harmonic rhythm is currently a global lever, but the classical gesture is local — one chord per bar in the phrase body, then two in the pre-cadence bar, ideally as a **cadential 6/4** (I⁶₄→V→I), which your cadence vocabulary lacks and which makes authentic cadences read as *prepared* rather than merely correct. **Bass-line planning at phrase scope**: you place approach tones per chord, but choosing inversions so the bass traverses a stepwise line across the whole phrase (the descending tetrachord under dramaturg withholding is practically begging to exist next to your dominant pedal) turns the bass from a root-reporter into a voice. **Single-peak contour planning**: enforce one melodic apex per phrase, approached and left by the gap-fill pattern (leap up, fill down stepwise); you already cap the ambit while withholding, so this is the complementary rule for normal operation. **Groove persistence as an explicit contract**: verify that percussion/arp pattern identity is held for a phrase under stable levers and varied only at fills — per-bar seeding makes this true only as long as the derived parameters don't wiggle, and it's worth pinning, because pattern identity is what makes harmonic change *legible*. And **guide-tone lines**: threading the 3rds and 7ths of successive chords into a slow connecting line is both a coherence check and a free scaffold to seed the countermelody from.

If I had to pick three to prototype first: the phrasing modifier (cheapest, biggest "performed" jump), periods (biggest coherence jump), and the countermelody with its linter rules (the polyphony you asked for, and the one that produces a reusable spec for the C-engine side, in keeping with how you've treated everything else in this project).
